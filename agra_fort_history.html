History

Agra Fort, located in Agra, India, is a historical fort that served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638. The fort is also known as the Red Fort of Agra due to its massive walls built from red sandstone.

Construction of the fort was started by the Mughal Emperor Akbar in 1565 and completed by his grandson Shah Jahan. Agra Fort was the site of numerous significant historical events, including the imprisonment of Shah Jahan by his son Aurangzeb.

Significance

Agra Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture in India. The fort comprises several impressive structures, including the Jahangir Palace, Khas Mahal, Diwan-i-Khas, and the beautiful Pearl Mosque.

Today, Agra Fort is a major tourist attraction, drawing visitors from around the world who come to marvel at its historical significance and architectural splendor.
