About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, an iconic symbol of love, is a stunning masterpiece of architecture located in Agra, India. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, it stands as a testament to their eternal love and devotion. Constructed over 22 years using white marble and adorned with intricate carvings and precious stones, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors worldwide, drawn by its timeless beauty and romantic allure.

Brief history of Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, constructed by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, is a renowned symbol of love. Built as a mausoleum for his wife, Mumtaz Mahal, its exquisite design blends Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural influences. Today, it stands as an enduring testament to love's enduring power and attracts countless visitors to admire its beauty
